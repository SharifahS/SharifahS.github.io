Project Summary

    An analysis of all reported crimes in Toronto from January 2014 to December 2019. The analysis was done with Pandas and the visualizations were created in Microsoft Power BI. Machine Learning was applied using Facebook Prophet to predict the future of reported crimes in Toronto.

    Slide 1 - The First slide focuses on the reported crimes by Police Divisions. This will give us an idea of what areas have the most reported crimes, or, by contrast, the least amount of reported crimes.

    Slide 2 -Here, the total number of crimes per year is displayed, making it clear for the reader that there has been a steady increase of reported crimes every year.

    Slide 3 - An interactive map was created in PowerBI giving users the ability to filter the location of all the crimes based on desired neighbourhoods.

    Slide 4 - The first pie graph shows a percent breakdown of all crimes by the type of offense reported. The second pie graph is a percent breakdown of crimes reported in each month.

    Slide 5 - This bar graph gives a breakdown of reported crime and the type of crime committed according to the day of the week when it was reported. Here you can see what crimes that are most popular on certain days of the week

    Slide 6 - This is a screencap of a dataset used to create the various graphs and visuals. It contained all general information of any reported crime in Toronto between the years 2014 and 2019.

    Slide 7 - The interactive dashboard allows the user to filter the data by type of crime that was reported and the premise type where the crime took place.

    Slide 8 - Visualization created in Facebook Prophet to show future crime patterns.
